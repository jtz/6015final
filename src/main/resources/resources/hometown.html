About Sichuan

My hometown is Sichuan Province located in southwest China. Traditionally, Sichuan is well-known for its picturesque scenery and gourmet cuisine, and it's always been a popular tourist destination for both domestic and foreign visitors. An old Chinese saying compares Sichuan to an earthly paradise or the Country of Heaven. Benefit from the moderate climate and the rich natural resource, people here live a happy leisure life.

Local Cuisine

Sichuan is well known for its spicy cuisine and use of Sichuan peppers due to its humid climate. We Sichuanese are proud of our cuisine, known as one of the Four Great Traditions of Chinese cuisine. An old saying goes "one dish, one shape, hundreds of dishes, hundreds of tastes" to describe its acclaimed diversity. The most prominent traits of Sichuanese cuisine are described by four words: spicy, hot, fresh and fragrant.

For me, the most attractive part of living Sichuan is the gourmet local cuisine. The local dishes that should not be missed are as follows:

Kung Pao chicken
a spicy, stir-fried dish made with cubes of chicken, peanuts, vegetables (traditionally Welsh onion only), and chili peppers
Mapo tofu
a bowl of tofu set in a spicy sauce based on douban and douchi, typically a thin, oily, and bright red suspension, along with minced meat
Dandan noodles
a noodle dish with a spicy sauce usually containing preserved vegetables, chili oil, Sichuan pepper, minced pork, and scallions served over noodles
Hot pot in Mala style
a simmering metal pot with broth at the center of a table, and all raw ingredients placed beside the metal pot are to be added and cooked in the broth

Tourist Attraction

Sichuan is one of the most popular cultural and tourist centers in the western China. The famous tourist attractions I recommend are Jiuzhaigou Valley, Huanglong Scenic and Historic Interest Area, and Mount Emei Scenic Area.

Jiuzhaigou Valley

Jiuzhaigou is a nature reserve and national park located in the north of Sichuan Province. It is known for its many multi-level waterfalls, colorful lakes, and snow-capped peaks. Its elevation ranges from 2,000 to 4,500 metres (6,600 to 14,800 ft). Click picture to learn more.

Huanglong Scenic and Historic Interest Area

Huanglong is a scenic and historic interest area located in the northwest of Sichuan Province. It is known for its colorful travertine pools formed by calcite deposits, especially in Huanglonggou (Yellow Dragon Gully), as well as diverse forest ecosystems, snow-capped peaks, waterfalls and hot springs. Huanglong is also home to many endangered species including the giant panda and the Sichuan golden snub-nosed monkey. Click picture to learn more.

Mount Emei Scenic Area

Mount Emei is a 3,099-meter-tall (10,167 ft) mountain in Sichuan Province and is the highest of the Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains of China. This is the location of the first Buddhist temple built in China in the 1st century CE. Click picture to learn more.
